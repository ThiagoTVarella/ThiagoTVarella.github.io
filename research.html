Research


My research explores the fundamental principles of animal behavior and learning, aiming to uncover shared mechanisms between humans and non-human animals. Early in my career, I investigated chaotic dynamical systems inspired by biophysical neuronal models and decision-making processes, including projects on time perception that informed my understanding of behavioral variability.

My current focus is on vocal development. I employ a Bayesian framework to model the stochastic dynamics underlying vocal learning, examining how the energy cost of vocalizing and the information conveyed shape developmental trajectories. In parallel, I leverage reinforcement learning to simulate self-motivated exploration and embodied learning, providing insights into how contextual factors such as parental feedback influence vocal behavior. This interdisciplinary work enhances my expertise in statistical methods and programming with Python and MATLAB.
